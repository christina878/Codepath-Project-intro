People at TEENAGERS

Acne is most common in teenagers because of the hormonal changes that occur during puberty. During puberty, the body produces more of the hormones testosterone and androgen. These hormones stimulate the sebaceous glands in the skin to produce more sebum, an oily substance that helps to protect and lubricate the skin.

People at MIDDLE AGE

Hormones: Acne is caused by hormonal changes, such as those that occur during puberty, menstruation, and pregnancy.

Peolpe OLD AGE 65+

Medications: Some medications, such as corticosteroids and lithium, can cause acne as a side effect.

OR

Medical conditions: Some medical conditions, such as polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) and Cushing's syndrome, can also cause acne.

Acne: Overview

Severe acne: People who have severe forms of acne have a lot of papules and pustules, as well as nodules on their skin. Acne mostly develops on areas of skin that have more oil glands, like the face, chest, back and shoulders. The main reason teenagers get acne is because their bodies make more androgen hormones during puberty. A number of scientific studies have looked into the effect of diet on acne, but they mostly didn't find any connection between what people eat and the likelihood of developing acne. People with acne often try out different things to improve the appearance of their skin.
